Contracts, NDAs, signed agreements, case files: legal documents routinely contain terms, figures, and personal details that none of the parties want sitting in a readable inbox. Whether you're a lawyer, a freelancer signing a contract, or a founder handling an NDA, here's how to send legal documents so only the intended party can open them.

## Why "just email it" is a problem

A signed agreement is a record of who agreed to what, often with names, addresses, financial terms, and signatures. Emailed as a plain attachment, it's readable by both mail providers and every backup, and forwarded copies multiply. For privileged or confidential material, that's a real exposure, and one you can't undo after the fact.

## Send it encrypted to the other party

1. Ask the other side to open [filekey.app](/) and tap **Send me a file** for their personal link.
2. Open the link, add the document, and approve with Face ID or Touch ID. It's encrypted on your device into a `.filekey` file only they can open.
3. Send it by whatever channel you already use. The contents stay private even when the channel isn't.

> Because each file is encrypted to a specific recipient, a forwarded copy is useless to anyone else. It can only be opened by the person you sent it to.

## For ongoing work

If you exchange documents with the same people regularly (a client, opposing counsel, a co-founder), FileKey remembers them as contacts after the first exchange, so sending the next document is quick. None of that changes the privacy: every file is still encrypted to that person's key alone.

The same applies to [tax documents](/blog/send-tax-documents-securely/), [medical records](/blog/share-medical-records-privately/), and anything else confidential.
