FileKey is built so that we cannot spy on you, even if we wanted to. This page explains what that means in practice.

## What we collect

Nothing. FileKey has no accounts, no analytics, no cookies, and no trackers. We do not log who you are or what you encrypt.

## Your files

All encryption and decryption happen in your browser, on your device. Your files and your keys never leave your device and are never uploaded to us. We could not read them if we tried, because we never receive them.

## This blog

The blog sets no cookies and runs no analytics. The only script on a blog page is the light and dark theme switch, and your theme preference is stored locally in your browser, not sent anywhere.

## Hosting

The site is served as static files by our hosting provider, which may keep standard, short-lived server logs (such as IP addresses) for security and abuse prevention, as nearly all web hosts do. We do not use those logs to identify or track you.

## Changes

If any of this ever changes, we will update this page. Future features, such as shareable links, would have their own clearly described behavior before you ever use them.
