If you handle other people's sensitive documents, tax forms, medical records, signed contracts, asking clients to email them is a liability you carry. With FileKey you can hand every client one link that encrypts whatever they send so only you can open it, with no account or app on their end.

## Why is collecting client documents by email risky?

An emailed attachment sits in readable form on your client's device, both mail providers, your inbox, and every backup of all of them, often for years. For a professional handling regulated or confidential data, that's exposure you don't control and can't undo, multiplied by every client and every file. A breach of any one inbox in that chain exposes the documents.

## How do you set up a secure intake link?

1. Open [filekey.app](/) and create or unlock your filekey (about 30 seconds, no account).
2. Open the **Your FileKey** menu (the sliders icon, top right) and choose **Receive a file**.
3. Copy the link it gives you, or tap **Show QR** for an in-person handoff. The link is tied to your key, so you can share it as widely as you like.

## What does the client experience?

They open your link and add a file. It's encrypted in their browser to you alone, with no account, no app, and no Face ID on their end. They get back a `.shared.filekey` file and send it to you however they normally would. Because it's already locked to your key before it leaves their device, the channel that carries it, even plain email, can't read it. Only you can open it, with your passkey.

> One honest limit: FileKey isn't a hosted upload portal yet. The client sends you the encrypted file over their own channel; you don't get an automatic upload into a dashboard. A hosted, zero-knowledge relay (a link the file lands in) is on the roadmap. Today the win is that the file is encrypted to you before it ever leaves the client.

## Where should you put your intake link?

- In your email signature, so every client always has it.
- On your website's contact or "send us your documents" page.
- As a QR code on intake paperwork or a business card.

The same link works for everyone, and every file that comes back is encrypted to you and only you.
