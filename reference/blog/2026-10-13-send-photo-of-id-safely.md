A bank, a marketplace, a rental, or an age check wants a photo of your driver's license or ID. That image is a ready-made identity document, and once it's in someone's inbox or a chat, you've lost control of it. Here's how to send it so only the requester can see it.

## Why an ID photo is risky

A photo of your ID carries your name, address, date of birth, ID number, and signature, everything needed to impersonate you or pass another verification check. Texted or emailed, it's readable on several servers and easy to forward, and you can't recall it.

## Send it encrypted

1. Ask the requester to open [filekey.app](/) and tap **Send me a file** for their link.
2. Open the link, add the photo, and approve with Face ID or Touch ID. It's encrypted on your device to that recipient only.
3. Send the `.filekey` file by any channel. Only they can open it.

> Two habits that help: watermark the image with the purpose and date ("For [company] verification only"), and crop to just what's asked. A watermarked, single-purpose copy is much harder to reuse.

The same goes for a [passport copy](/blog/send-passport-copy-safely/) or [your Social Security number](/blog/send-social-security-number-securely/).
